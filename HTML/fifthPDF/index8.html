AKMS

Description

The AKMS, introduced in the 1950s, is a folding-stock variant of the AK-47 designed by Mikhail Kalashnikov. The name stands for "Avtomat Kalashnikova Modernizirovanniy Skladnoy," meaning "Kalashnikov Automatic Rifle Modernized Folding." It was created for airborne troops and vehicle crews, prioritizing compactness and portability. Like the AK-47, it uses a gas-operated, rotating bolt mechanism and fires 7.62x39mm ammunition.
